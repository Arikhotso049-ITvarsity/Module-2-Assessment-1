KOALA

Koalas are marsupials native to eastern Australia, famous for their fluffy ears and spoon-shaped noses. They are arboreal, spending most of their time in eucalyptus trees, which also make up almost their entire diet. Koalas are generally solitary animals, communicating through soft grunts and bellows.

Adult koalas have thick, grey fur and strong, sharp claws for climbing trees. Their specialized digestive system allows them to eat eucalyptus leaves, which are tough and low in nutrients. Koalas sleep up to 18â€“20 hours a day to conserve energy.

Koalas live in eucalyptus forests and woodlands, usually near water sources. Due to habitat loss and bushfires, they are considered vulnerable, and conservation efforts are ongoing.
