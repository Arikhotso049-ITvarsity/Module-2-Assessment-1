TIGER

Tigers are the largest members of the cat family, belonging to the genus Panthera. They are known for their powerful bodies, sharp teeth, and distinctive orange coats with black stripes. Each tigerâ€™s stripe pattern is unique.

Tigers are native to parts of Asia and can be found in forests, grasslands, mangroves, and wetlands. They are solitary and territorial animals, spending most of their time alone except during mating or when a mother is raising her cubs.

Tigers are carnivorous hunters that rely on stealth and strength to catch prey such as deer and wild boar. Although they are strong swimmers, they prefer dense cover where they can ambush their prey.
